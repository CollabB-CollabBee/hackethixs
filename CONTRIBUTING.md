# Contributor Agreement (Voluntary Pre‑Funding Phase)

## 1. Nature of Participation
By contributing to this project, you acknowledge that:

* All contributions are fully voluntary.

* There is no financial compensation available at this stage.

* No promises, guarantees, or expectations of future payment, employment, or contracting can be made.

* Your participation does not create an employment, contractor, or partnership relationship.

This clarity protects both contributors and the project.

## 2. Purpose of the Project
This project is in an early development phase, combining:

* a personal research and design initiative

* a formal grant application trajectory

* community‑driven experimentation

Your contributions help shape the platform, but they do not create ownership claims or financial entitlements.

## 3. Contribution Expectations
Contributors are expected to:

* engage respectfully and collaboratively

* communicate availability and limitations

* contribute only when they genuinely want to avoid joining with expectations of compensation or future paid roles

Inactive contributors may be removed to keep the project manageable.

## 4. Intellectual Property
Unless otherwise stated:

* You retain rights to your own original work.

* You grant the project a non‑exclusive, perpetual license to use your contributions within the platform.

* You may request removal of your contributions if they are not yet integrated into core functionality.

This ensures fairness and avoids exploitation.

## 5. Transparency and Boundaries
The project commits to:

* transparency about funding status

* clear communication about expectations

* avoiding coercive, manipulative, or hierarchical dynamics

* protecting contributors from false promises or pressure

Contributors commit to:

* respecting the voluntary nature of the project

* asking questions early if anything is unclear

* withdrawing if the structure no longer fits their needs

## 6. Removal of Access
The project may remove contributor access if:

* the contributor becomes inactive

* expectations are misaligned

* the contributor requests removal

* the project enters a new phase requiring a different structure

This is not punitive; it is operational hygiene.

## 7. Future Funding
If future grants or funding are secured:

* roles will be defined transparently

* contributors may apply, but no priority or entitlement is implied

* voluntary contributions do not convert into paid obligations

This ensures compliance with regulations and ethical clarity.
